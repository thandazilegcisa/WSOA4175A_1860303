What is Decolonisation?


To answer this i would like to first point the compound expression that Mingnolo & Walsh introduce: The colonial matrix of power. They define this matrix as the convergence of modernity and coloniality. And considering the fact that this matrix of power is plural in nature they consider it important that readers, seek to define what decolonisation means to them. This is particularly interesting because by so doing they priortise geo-political relative truths, instead of an absolute truth about what decolonisation means to the formerly colonised. This is important because this signifies the ideals of decoloniality, which is to not only consider absolute truths presented by western civilisations as valid but instead consider other ways and forms of cultivating knowledge, without necessary eliminating and blindly accepting western thought.

This is where the idea of modernity comes in, this considers that western thought and civilisation through the lens of modernity is in most of us. Therefore, for me considering my geo-political standing in modern day South Afrcia. I would like to discuss and explore decolonisation through the lens of cultural heritage in an effor to see myself clearly. Now since the dawn colonisation, separation, segregation acts defined the mobility of the formerly colonised. And subsequently since the dawn of the industrial revolution the migration to urban centers and towns/townships away form traditional homesteads has brought about elements of modernity into the South African lives. And considering that modernity is a way of living it means that it is a practice and custom.

To put this convergence into perspective i will use the following example. In Xhosa tradition boys ordained into manhood by performing traditional method of circumcision that is a part of a whole ceremony that is not just exclusive to circumcision but other beliefs and customs, although this project will not necessary discuss these practices. I only seek to highlight how the modernity of daily life and use of modern medicine (Anesthetics) results/ could potentially result in being othered by the people of the culture despite sharing the same heritage and language,thus because of this element of modernity and coloniality, in the face of culture one would not be seen as a true Xhosa man.

So in light of this i though it would be interesting to interrogate the act of "seeing myself clearly" in relation to otherselves with whom we share the universe and particularly culture for this project. Because with the example in consideration i am in a position where i am modernised and colonised by the practices that i've taken and not taken in the view of culture. And consequently i cannot necessary eliminate my modernity. Therefore, decolonisation becomes a very important concept/practice to consider, and as such since this perspective is about culture and heritage, I will interrogate decolonisation from the perspective of participating in practicing art as heritage through and on the internet.
