A Formal Analysis of Click Club


Net Art much like the internet is no singular and obsolete object. Insead it is plural, it can come in the form of using the internet as means to curate interaction and how an artwork is experienced. Hence, Click Club is an art project by Peter Luining, that consists of a webpage with a block of 3 rows of images that all link to new pages with animations,images, interactive pages. Elements of this art work particularly reminds me of the story of the library of babel.In the book, the authour writes about a universe if books that has been and will be created. The library of babel has no limits, which is indicated in the why click club structures how it exhibits the aura of the artwork. The artwork almost feels as though it is endless, which exemplifies the very medium that the creator uses.

Each click feels like and adventure into the unknown which sort of the artwork as a type of point and click adventure artwork that uses only a collage of images instead of text in this case. Therefore, the artwork from my perspective satirises the internet and things that could be viewed as click bait. Click bait is content whose main intent is to distract or attract attention and encourage visitors to click on a link to a particular page, which reinforces the idea of "adventure" envisioned through the art work.

However, the artwork makes use of a weakly interactive input - clicking and despite this it still manages to add a bit of randomness through the use of its on hover function which makes for good click bait, because most click baits eventually send you into a rabitt hole of "content" that you did not even ask for.

Therefore, what makes Click Club interesting is how it uses the affordances provided by the browser and sort of signifying its own domain. I particularly liked all the gifs and animations used throughout the website because it also exmplifies the how gifs and memes are the vernacular of the internet, which is why i am able to deduce the kind of connotations that i now present. Because if the artwork was static it probably would'nt have signified the same representations.
